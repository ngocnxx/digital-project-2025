Floating Markets

Floating markets are the heart of the Mekong. Cai Rang and Phong Dien are packed with boats loaded with fruit, vegetables, and even breakfast noodles. Sellers call out their wares while buyers paddle up in tiny wooden canoes.


It‚Äôs a colorful, noisy, and joyful morning tradition. Don‚Äôt miss tasting fresh pineapple, a bowl of hot rice noodles or sipping strong coffee from a boat-side ‚Äúcafe‚Äù! This is a unique experience you cannot find anywhere else.

Village life in the Mekong is simple, peaceful, and welcoming. Rice paddies, coconut groves, and flower farms stretch in every direction, dotted with small stilt houses and bamboo bridges.


Villagers usually invite you in for home-cooked meals or teach you to row a boat. Hospitality here is legendary, you may expect smiles and a slower pace of life. Opposed to the bustling city like Ho Chi Minh City, life here will offer you another view of the simplicity of Mekong's people.
