Da Nang – Hue – Hoi An

Ancient Heritage and Coastal Charm of Vietnam

Step into Central Vietnam’s golden triangle, a region steeped in imperial history, blessed with spectacular beaches, and glowing with colorful lanterns at night. Da Nang, Hue, and Hoi An each have their own color, but together create a travel journey that’s unforgettable.

Start your morning walking Hue’s ancient citadel, savor Mi Quang noodles by Da Nang’s riverside by noon, and by evening you’ll be wandering Hoi An’s lantern-lit streets—all in a single day. History, food, beaches, festivals, it’s all here, wrapped in Central Vietnam.

Weather & Temperature

Central Vietnam’s weather is famously diverse with sunny beaches in Da Nang, misty mornings in Hue, and warm breezes by Hoi An’s rivers. The dry season (Feb–Aug) brings blue skies and sparkling sea, perfect for exploring and relaxing alike.

Imperial Citadel

Hue was once Vietnam’s imperial capital, home to the Nguyen Dynasty emperors and grand palaces. The sprawling citadel and royal tombs are filled with history and centuries of stories. Walking these ancient corridors is like stepping into a living museum, you get a chance to explore Vietnam's past dynasty and the infrastructure they've built.


UNESCO-listed and lovingly preserved, the citadel’s moats, lotus ponds, and ornate gates are perfect for history lovers, photographers, and anyone chasing old-world beauty.

Da Nang give a youthful energy to Central Vietnam. Modern bridges, sleek riverfronts, and a coastline that stretches for miles make it the region’s most dynamic city. Golden Bridge at Ba Na Hills, held by giant stone hands, is a new icon that’s captured imaginations worldwide.


At night, Dragon Bridge breathes fire and light, while locals and travelers gather at night markets. Here, tradition and innovation with street food stalls beside skyscrapers, ancient pagodas near trendy cafes. It’s fusion of Central Vietnam.

Lantern Festival

Every night, Hoi An transforms into a glowing wonderland with thousands of colorful silk lanterns. The ancient town is famous for its UNESCO heritage streets, wooden shophouses, tailor shops, and riverside markets.


Join the monthly Full Moon Lantern Festival,release a candle-lit lantern on the Thu Bon River for good luck. It’s the heart of Central Vietnam’s romance and beauty, where tradition is alive in every glow.

Bun Bo Hue (Spicy Beef Noodle Soup)

Central Vietnamese cuisine is famous for its spicy, bold flavors and artistic presentation. Bun Bo Hue is a highlight—rich beef broth, thick rice noodles, lemongrass, and a fiery kick of chili oil.

Try Mi Quang, a turmeric-yellow noodle dish topped with shrimp, pork, peanuts, and crispy rice crackers. Cao Lau from Hoi An brings chewy noodles and savory pork, with a flavor you’ll only find here.

Every meal is a celebration of local produce, fresh herbs, and centuries-old recipes. Central Vietnam’s markets and riverside stalls are heaven for food lovers—so come hungry, and leave happy!
