General Information Before Departure

Photo obtained from Pixabay

Currency

The official currency is the Vietnamese Dong (₫). While credit cards are accepted in major cities, cash is preferred in rural areas, markets, and street food stalls. Make sure you always carry cash for convenience. Note: ATMs are widespread and support foreign cards however, mostly common in cities (e.g. Ho Chi Minh City). Do not recommend to bring only card to rural areas.

Language

Vietnamese is the national language. English is common in cities and tourist spots, but limited in rural areas. Learning key phrases like "Xin chào" (hello) and "Cảm ơn" (thank you) is appreciated. Note: English signs and speakers are found in cities, but in places like the Mekong Delta, a traveller guide or basic Vietnamese may be needed.

Power & Plugs

Vietnam uses Type A, C, D sockets (110/220 V). Universal adapters are recommended and can be purchased at convenience stores like 7-Eleven throughout the country. Note: Preferably purchase adapters BEFORE travelling. Otherwise please purchase in reliable supermarket or bookstores to ensure safety.

Best Time to Visit

South Vietnam don't have four seasons but only two - Dry & Wet. Dry season (Nov–Apr): 28–32°C with clear skies and minimal rainfall. This would be perfect if you prefer dry weather for beaches or travelling. While wet season (May–Oct): showers throughout the day and sometimes can lead to floods. This would be the best you if enjoy a cooler weather with droplets of rain (like Hue, the city of rain in Vietnam).

Regions Overview

Ho Chi Minh City

Ho Chi Minh City is the main metropolis of Vietnam. It is the heart of Vietnam's economy, with over 10 millions people as its population. Ho Chi Minh (previously Saigon), is one of the main tourist attraction, as it's unique for the combination of all cultures across Vietnam.

Mekong Delta

Mekong Delta is gifted with beautiful rivers and landscapes, with many of its unique rice fields and dishes. This is the place where you want to find peace instead of bustling cities, close you eyes and hear the sound of wind whistling through the rice paddy. Here, you can explore the simplicity yet full of love from the West of Vietnam's people.

Hue - Da Nang - Hoi An

Hue - Da Nang - Hoi An, once the home of the Vietnamese Emperor before 1945, now has transformed into popular tourists attractions due to its picturesque beaches, historical landmarks and vintage latern festival by the rivers at night. Contrast of the others, Hue- Da Nang - Hoi An reflects a more old, ancient and historical reflections of Vietnam's past history and culture.
