Welcome to South Vietnam...

Welcome to my website - your go to guide for exploring the vibrant and unforgettable South Vietnam. Here, you’ll find things you need to plan a trip packed with culture, adventure, and delicious food.

So why South Vietnam? It’s a region where bustling cities meet peaceful rivers, full of history and context. Street food is unmatched. Every corner has a story and tradition to tell. From the energy of Ho Chi Minh City to the lush landscapes of the Mekong Delta and the ancient buildings (mixed with beaches) of Hue-Da Nang-Hoi An, South Vietnam offers a perfect mix of excitement and relaxation. This site helps you discover the best spots, culture, cuisine, and must-go landmarks - all in one place. South Vietnam includes all parts of the South and Central Vietnam.

General Information Before Departure

Photo obtained from Pixabay

Currency

The official currency is the Vietnamese Dong (₫). While credit cards are accepted in major cities, cash is still preferred in rural areas, markets, and food stalls. Make sure you carry cash for convenience.
Note: ATMs are everywhere in cities but rare in small towns. Don’t bring only cards to rural areas.

Language

Vietnamese is the national language. English is common in cities, but limited in rural areas. Learning key phrases like "Xin chào" (hello) and "Cảm ơn" (thank you) is appreciated.
Note: English signs and speakers are found in cities, but in the Mekong Delta, you might need a guide or basic Vietnamese.

Power & Plugs

Vietnam uses Type A, C, D sockets (110/220 V). Bring a universal adapter or buy one when you arrive. Note: Buy adapters in reputable stores to ensure safety.

Best Time to Visit

South Vietnam has only two seasons - Dry (Nov–Apr), 28–32°C, clear skies, and Wet (May–Oct) with showers. Pick what suits your travel style.
